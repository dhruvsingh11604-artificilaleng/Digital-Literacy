Do’s
	Don’ts
	Audit your privacy settings regularly to ensure you know exactly who can see your personal posts.
	Post while highly emotional or angry. Rants often feel good in the moment but can lead to long-term regret.
	Network professionally on platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni and industry leaders.
	Post illegal or policy-violating content, such as underage drinking or academic dishonesty (cheating).
	Think before you tag. Ask friends for permission before posting photos of them, and expect the same in return.
	Engage with "trolls" or online bullies. It’s better to block and move on than to damage your reputation in a public spat.
	Share your wins. Post about projects, internships, and volunteer work to build a positive personal brand.
	Complain about professors or employers. Even if you're right, it can make you look difficult to work with.
	Fact-check before sharing. Make sure the information or news you repost is accurate to maintain your credibility.
	Overshare sensitive data, like your physical address, current exact location, or student ID number.
